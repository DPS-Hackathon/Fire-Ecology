Organisations

PyroLife

PyroLife is a European Union-funded project that trains early-stage researchers in various aspects of fire ecology and management. It aims to build a new generation of experts equipped to address the complex challenges of fire-prone landscapes.

IAWF

IAWF stands for International Association of Wildland Fire. It is a global network of experts, researchers, and professionals dedicated to advancing the knowledge and management of wildland fires. They provide resources, conferences, and collaboration opportunities for those involved in fire ecology.

Fire with EcoSystems

Mosaic Patterns

Wildland fires create a mixture of totally burned, partially burned, and unburned sections called a burn mosaic. The varying degrees of burn are a result of many factors including wind shifts, daily temperature changes, moisture levels, and varying chemical composition of the vegetation. The burn mosaic results in varied regrowth rates that creates a vegetation mosaic.

Soil

Wildland fires can be both a detriment and a benefit to soil. The soil can become more nutrientrich after a fire due to the high mineral content of the ash and charcoal and also due to the warm, moist conditions that increase microbial activity. The intense heat can also cause soil particles to become water-repellant, causing rainwater to run off. As the water runs off it can carry soil particles with it and lead to erosion.
